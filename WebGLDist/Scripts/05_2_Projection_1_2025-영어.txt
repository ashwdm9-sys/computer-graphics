The clipping process is the process of separating the inner
and outer parts of a triangle that overlaps the boundary of the clipping volume
and drawing only the inner part
In the left picture, only the parts of the triangles inside the clipping volume are visible,

Because we need to draw a 3D defined object on a 2D screen,
a 3D to 2D mapping is required
This process is called projection
As you can see in the picture,
points are projected at the intersections of the projectors and the view plane
Here, the projector is a straight line
connecting the COP, that is, the position of the camera, and a point on the object
There are two types of projection
First, parallel projection is when the COP is infinitely far away
All projectors are parallel
In this case, the size ratio of the object is maintained and projected
Because there is no sense of perspective, it is different from the real world we live in
Parallel projection is used for machine design
that requires accuracy in size or distance
In perspective projection, the COP exists at a finite distance
A more realistic projection method
Objects that are far away are projected smaller and objects that are close are projected larger,
so the actual size ratio is not maintained
In computer graphics, most people use Perspective Projection
Parallel Projection is simple
Just keep the X and Y coordinates of the 3D coordinates and ignore the Z coordinate

Assume that Point Pc = (Xc, Yc, Zc) exists in camera space
